## Update: Still Running CachyOS

It has been a while since my first impressions, and I’m happy to report that [CachyOS](https://cachyos.org/) has officially become my "forever" build. 

As an engineer, there is a specific satisfaction in finding a [rolling release](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rolling_release) distribution that evolves with you. I’ve reached a point where my system configuration is so dialed in that I consider this my **lifetime build**.

### Performance and Stability
The [nightly builds](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Software_release_life_cycle#Nightly) have been incredibly reliable. The "out-of-the-box" performance tuning that the CachyOS team implements—specifically their optimized [kernel](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Linux_kernel) and scheduler tweaks—continues to impress me. It remains, without question, the smoothest desktop experience I’ve had on Linux to date.

### The Gaming Landscape
Gaming on Linux has made massive strides, and CachyOS makes it even easier to leverage [Wine](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wine_(software)) and [Proton](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Proton_(software)) to bridge the gap.

*   **The Wins:** Most of my library runs flawlessly. The overhead is negligible, and the system responsiveness while gaming is top-tier.
*   **The Frustrations:** I still hold a bit of hope for better kernel-level [anti-cheat](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Anti-cheat_software) compatibility. Titles like *Rust* remain elusive due to their reliance on specific Windows-only drivers, which is the only real barrier keeping me from a 100% Windows-free life.
*   **Connectivity:** I’ve found [Bluetooth](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bluetooth) performance to be perfectly acceptable. It is occasionally "spotty," but honestly? It’s no worse—and often better—than what I experience on Windows.

### The "Ableton Problem"
As I continue to balance my engineering work with my creative output, the lack of native [Ableton Live](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ableton_Live) support remains the biggest hurdle. 

While I’ve looked into alternatives like [Reaper](https://www.reaper.fm/), Ableton is so deeply woven into my creative workflow that I haven't fully committed to the switch yet. 

For now, my studio setup remains a slightly more complex affair.

### Customization: The End Game
The real magic of CachyOS for me is the ability to customize every layer of the stack. I love tweaking [KDE Plasma](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/KDE_Plasma_5) to match a specific aesthetic, the level of control is unparalleled. 

It feels great to have a machine that is a true extension of my workflow rather than a walled garden.
