# Forget About FAANG

For most engineers, being concerned about working for a [FAANG](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Big_Tech) company isn't an option. The roles are highly competitive to get into and once you're there, job security isn't a guarantee even during periods of [economic expansion.](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Economic_expansion)

Statistically speaking, you are not likely to work at a FAANG company, but it is an achievable goal for those with the drive.

I'm going to break down some data on the US tech job market and argue why the average engineer shouldn't concern themselves with FAANG companies.
### Analysis

According to the BLS Number of Jobs<sup>1</sup> statistic, there are 1,895,500 [software developer/QA jobs](https://www.bls.gov/ooh/computer-and-information-technology/software-developers.htm) reported in 2024. We can use that number as our denominator in our next calculation.

For our numerator, we can estimate the number of engineers at FAANG companies. **Meta, Apple, Amazon, Netflix, and Alphabet/Google** don't publicly release precise, up-to-the-minute US software engineer counts. 

So, we'll use the most recent available data and estimates, focusing on engineering/tech departments, and we'll be generous with the numbers to provide a high-end estimate.

| Company      | Estimated Global Engineering/Tech Headcount | Source Notes                                                                 |
|--------------|---------------------------------------------|------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| Meta (FB)    | 25,900                                      | Estimated 35% of ~74,067 total employees are Software Engineers/Tech.        |
| Apple (A)    | 49,800                                      | Apple's largest department is Engineering (approx. 41% of global workforce). |
| Amazon (A)   | 234,000                                     | Estimated 15% of total workforce are in "AWS & Technology."                  |
| Netflix (N)  | 2,500                                       | Very small engineering headcount estimated ~2,000 to ~3,000 engineers.       |
| Alphabet (G) | 60,000                                      | Conservative estimate, given total workforce is ~183,000.                    |
| **TOTAL**    | 372,200                                     | A generous global estimate of FAANG technical roles.                         |

><sup> 1</sup> *Number of Jobs: The employment, or size, of this occupation in 2024, which is the base year of the 2024–34 employment projections.*
